AI Discovery

Identify high‑value AI opportunities and prepare your data for a successful transformation.

Why Start with Discovery?

Before investing in AI, it’s critical to define what success looks like. Many small and medium‑sized businesses feel pressure to adopt AI because competitors are doing so, yet the most successful projects begin with clear business objectives. Ask yourself what outcomes you’re trying to achieve: do you need to speed up document processing, improve marketing campaigns or deliver better customer support? Framing the conversation around outcomes ensures that AI investments map directly to business priorities.

With adoption accelerating—77 percent of small businesses worldwide already using AI in at least one function—the discovery phase is your opportunity to focus on the problems that matter most. A structured approach avoids shiny‑object syndrome and lays the foundation for measurable ROI.

Steps in the Discovery Process

  1. Define objectives: Align AI initiatives with your strategic goals. Prioritize the outcomes that will have the biggest impact on customers, employees or revenues.
  2. Identify high‑impact areas: Look for repetitive, time‑consuming tasks where automation can deliver quick wins. Common examples include lead scoring, invoice matching, demand forecasting and support triage.
  3. Assess data readiness: No AI project succeeds without strong data governance. Distinguish between shareable information (like FAQs and product data) and confidential records (such as financial or health information). Ensure your data is clean, accessible and compliant with privacy regulations.
  4. Evaluate the landscape: Survey available AI tools and platforms. Decide whether a turnkey solution or a custom model trained on your own data will deliver more value.
  5. Plan for security and ethics: Address data privacy and potential biases up front. Transparency builds trust with customers and employees.

Common Use Cases for Small Businesses

AI isn’t a one‑size‑fits‑all technology. Identify use cases that align with your goals and data. According to recent surveys, marketing (53 percent), sales (49 percent) and customer support (46 percent) are the most common areas where small businesses deploy AI. Here are just a few examples:

Marketing & Sales

Use AI to personalize campaigns, optimize ad spend and score leads. Predictive analytics can surface buying patterns and improve conversion rates.

Customer Support

Deploy chatbots to answer routine questions instantly and route complex issues to human agents. Many businesses plan to integrate chatbots by 2025.

Operations

Forecast demand, schedule maintenance and optimize workflows. AI‑driven predictive analytics help reduce inventory stockouts and downtime, as illustrated in our case studies.

Finance & Administration

Automate invoice matching, expense classification and document processing to free your team for strategic work.
